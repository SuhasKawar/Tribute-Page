MAHESHWAR RAJWADA

Description: Maheshwar Rajwada was the centre of all her activities. At the entrance, the two wooden statues of the elephant and the horse are symbolic of the Holkars' dynasty reflecting Grandeur and the Military might.

MEETING PLACE OF MAHARANI AHILYABAI

Description: Inside, the two storeyed Rajwada has pillared verandahs on all sides with a central Courtyard. The upper floors served as the residence of the Maharani and other members of the royal family. The verandahs were meant for meetings held by Maharani Ahilyabai.

MAHARANI'S ROYAL SEAL

Description: While governing the Holkar kingdom, Maharani Ahilyabai had dedicated the State to Lord Shiva and had declared that She would manage the affairs of the State as a custodian. All Royal proclamations were signed by her alongwith 'SHRI SHANKAR'. HUZUR SHRI SHANKAR ORDER was like the masthead of Holkar rule.

MAHARANI AHILYABAI PALANQUIN AND SWORDS AND SHIELD USED BY HER SOLDIERS

Description: Maharani Ahilyabai had gained military training and was a skilled archer. She fought her invaders fiercely armed with swords and weapons and led the Holkar army into the battlefield.
